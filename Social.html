 🌞☄️ Formation of the Sun and Planets–One Big Story!

Around 4.6 billion years ago, our solar system was just a huge cloud of gas and dust floating in space. Everything we see today—the Sun, planets, moons, asteroids—all formed from this single cloud!

The Solar Nebula – The Beginning of Everything ☁️

  • A giant cloud of gas and dust (called a solar nebula) existed in space.
  • A shockwave from a nearby exploding star (a supernova) disturbed the cloud.
  • This caused the nebula to collapse under its own gravity and start spinning.

    • Birth of the Sun – The Center of It All 🌞

  • As the nebula spun, most of the gas gathered at the center.
  • The gas became hot and dense, eventually starting nuclear fusion-this what makes the Sun shine! 😊☀️
  • This is how our Sun was born, becoming a massive ball of burning hydrogen!

    • Formation of Planets🪐

  • The remaining gas and dust did not fall into the Sun. Instead, it spread out into a flat disk around it.
  • Small particles started sticking together to form bigger and bigger objects.
  • Over millions of years, these clumps became the planets.
    • Why Are Planets So Different? Inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars)
  • Formed close to the Sun, where it was too hot for gas and ice.
  • Made mostly of rock and metal.
    • Outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune)
  • Formed farther away, where it was cold enough for gases like hydrogen and helium to stay.
  • Became huge gas and ice giants.

    • So this is how the Sun☀️ and Planets🪐 formed
